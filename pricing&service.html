Frequently Asked Questions

How do project timelines work? ▼

Timelines depend on project scope and complexity. Starter projects typically take 2-4 weeks, Premium projects 4-8 weeks, and Enterprise solutions 8-16+ weeks. We provide a detailed timeline after an initial consultation.

Do you offer revisions and support? ▼

Yes! All projects include unlimited revisions during development. Post-launch support varies by plan: Starter includes 30 days, Premium includes 90 days, and Enterprise includes ongoing 24/7 support.

Can I upgrade or combine service plans? ▼

Absolutely. Many clients combine services (e.g., WordPress + DevOps). We offer package discounts and can create custom bundled proposals based on your specific needs.

What payment terms are available? ▼

We accept upfront payment, 50/50 splits (upfront + completion), or monthly installments for large projects. Custom payment plans available for Enterprise clients.

Do you provide hosting and maintenance? ▼

We can recommend and set up hosting partners, or you can use your own. Maintenance packages are available separately (included with Enterprise plans) starting at $300/month.

What's your refund or satisfaction policy? ▼

For our Starter plans, our satisfaction policy guarantees that the delivered solution strictly matches the specific scope, data, and requirements agreed upon before the project launched. Because these are rapid, high-efficiency rollouts, changes outside the initial agreement are not covered. For all contract-based tiers, once a contract period concludes, any new feature additions, architectural expansions, or modifications will be scoped separately and billed as a project extra depending on complexity.

Why are some service tracks (like WordPress) so much cheaper than others? ▼

Different platforms require completely different skill sets, execution times, and architectures. WordPress utilizes pre-built ecosystems and visual customizers, which allows us to deliver high-quality sites rapidly and affordably. Full-Coding, AI Automations, and DevOps require bespoke software engineering, manual database architecture, custom API piping, and infrastructure management, which commands a premium engineering rate.

Can we hire you to integrate directly into our internal engineering or product team? ▼

Yes! For scaling startups and enterprise teams, I offer fractional and dedicated contract arrangements. I can integrate seamlessly into your existing Slack, Jira, and GitHub workflows to work directly alongside your internal developers on complex feature rollouts or infrastructure overhauls.

Can you join or consult on my Blockchain / Web3 project? ▼

Absolutely. I can assist with frontend Web3 integrations, decentralized application (dApp) user interfaces, wallet connection components (like RainbowKit or Web3Modal), and connecting your custom web applications seamlessly to smart contract APIs.

Are project prices negotiable? ▼

Our tier prices are fixed to match the precise market value, overhead, and engineering hours required to execute them safely. However, we can always discuss adjusting the scope of a project—removing or adding specific deliverables—to build a custom roadmap that fits closer to your specific allocation budget.

Can I bring my own pre-purchased theme or UI template? ▼

Yes. If you have already purchased a specific premium WordPress theme or a frontend UI template design, we can use that as our development baseline. We will handle the heavy lifting of cleaning up the code, optimizing performance, setting up the layout architecture, and making it production-ready.

How does the 'On-Demand Support' billing work for DevOps? ▼

For standalone infrastructure tasks, we jump in, discuss the technical issue, and give you a clear quote between $20 and $600 depending on the complexity. Once approved, we fix it immediately. There are no ongoing commitments or hidden fees for individual tasks.

What are the terms for the monthly DevOps contracts? ▼

Our monthly DevOps tiers run on a flexible, month-to-month agreement. You get continuous cloud monitoring, security hardening, and pipeline management without being locked into a rigid long-term commitment. You can scale your tier up, down, or pause services with a 30-day notice.

Are API costs included in the Automation and AI packages? ▼

No. To keep our development rates fair and transparent, any external subscription or consumption costs (such as OpenAI API tokens, Zapier premium plans, or AWS cloud hosting) are billed directly to your own accounts. We help you set everything up securely under your own ownership.

Can I combine a website build with a monthly automation plan? ▼

Absolutely! Many clients start with a Custom-Coded or WordPress website build and attach a 'Basic Workflows' or 'Custom AI Integration' plan to instantly route incoming leads into their CRMs, email tools, or Slack teams.

What happens if a project requires more time than originally estimated? ▼

If a custom build runs into unforeseen technical hurdles or third-party API limitations on our end, we cover the extra development hours at no additional cost to you. However, if the timeline extends because of delayed content handoffs, shifting requirements, or feature creep from your side, we will pause and re-scope the project budget accordingly.

How do we track project milestones and communication? ▼

We prioritize complete transparency. Depending on your preference, we set up a dedicated workspace in Notion, Trello, or Jira where you can track tasks in real-time. Direct communication is handled cleanly via Slack, Discord, or scheduled weekly email updates so you always know exactly what phase your project is in.

Do you handle data compliance and security (like GDPR/CCPA)? ▼

Yes. Our Premium and Enterprise tiers across Full-Coding, Automation, and DevOps explicitly include security engineering. This covers setting up compliant cookie consents, implementing secure data tokenization so user data is never leaked, and configuring server firewalls to meet modern web privacy standards.

How does the final project sign-off and deployment handoff work? ▼

Once development is complete, we host the project in a private staging environment for your final review and testing. Once you give the official sign-off, we execute the live production deployment (DNS routing, server provisioning, database connection). Following deployment, you receive comprehensive walkthrough video documentation and full ownership transfer.

What happens if a third-party platform (like Zapier, Shopify, or OpenAI) changes its API or goes down? ▼

While we build on top of industry-standard infrastructure, we do not control third-party networks. If an external platform updates its security API, changes its pricing structure, or experiences a global outage, any necessary code updates or workflow reconfiguration required to fix your system will be scoped and billed as a separate maintenance task.

Do you write clean, commented code that another developer can easily take over? ▼

Absolutely. All custom-coded projects and automated scripts follow modern industry standards, clean architectural patterns, and semantic naming conventions. Everything is thoroughly commented and documented, meaning any standard software engineer can easily pick up where we left off without having to rewrite your platform from scratch.

Can you work with our existing hosting provider, or do we have to switch? ▼

We can deploy directly to your existing infrastructure (such as your current AWS, Google Cloud, DigitalOcean, or Hostinger accounts), provided it meets the minimal technical dependencies required for your project. If your current server setup is insecure or outdated, we will recommend a migration path during our strategy phase.

How do you protect our proprietary data and internal corporate passwords during development? ▼

Security is non-negotiable. We never hardcode passwords or sensitive API credentials directly into your codebase. Instead, we utilize encrypted environment variable managers and secure vault configurations. Additionally, we are completely open to signing a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) before you share any internal operations or databases with us.

Do you provide graphic design, or do I need to bring my own mockups? ▼

For our Full-Coding and WordPress tiers, our development packages include professional UI/UX layout engineering based on high-converting web frameworks. However, if your brand requires custom digital illustrations, branding logo creation, or ultra-bespoke 3D assets, you will need to provide those or hire a dedicated graphic designer to supply them before development begins.

What is your policy on 'feature creep' during an active project build? ▼

Feature creep refers to adding new ideas, pages, or automations that weren't in our initial signed agreement. To stay on track and hit our deadlines, any new feature requested mid-project will be put into a 'Phase 2 backlog.' We will finish the initial agreed-upon build first, then quote the Phase 2 additions separately.

Do you handle SEO (Search Engine Optimization) writing and content creation? ▼

We handle the technical SEO framework—this means configuring rapid page load speeds, clean indexing code structures, automatic sitemaps, and standard meta-tag architectures so Googlebot can read your site perfectly. However, writing the actual blog posts, sales copy, and marketing text is your responsibility, though we can recommend professional copywriters if needed.

Can you build a project using a specific language or framework not listed on your stack? ▼

Our core frameworks are selected because they deliver the absolute highest speed, security, and scalability for our clients. If your enterprise system strictly mandates an outside technology stack, we can discuss it during a custom strategy consultation to see if it aligns with our engineering capabilities.

How much input or time will you need from me during the development process? ▼

We respect your time and keep our process highly streamlined. We require a deep-dive onboarding kick-off at the beginning to gather asset files, data keys, and specifications. After that, we handle the heavy lifting independently, only reaching out for milestone approvals or critical access permissions during our scheduled weekly syncs.

What exactly counts as a 'micro-bug' under the On-Demand DevOps tier? ▼

Micro-bugs include quick infrastructure anomalies that can be resolved within a focused window—such as a broken SSL certificate renewal, a mismatched environment variable connection string, a basic domain routing error, or a simple server-side permission conflict. Large-scale database corruptions or complete cluster crashes fall under custom architecture troubleshooting.
